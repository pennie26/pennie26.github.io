## Chapter 2: Devil
So. Hello. My name is Devil. If you’re not Yugi herself, then I’m pretty sure you’ve never seen me. I’m kind of socially awkward, so being able to see me means you’re either in trouble or Yugi is finally trusting someone enough. Which I don’t think it’ll ever happen with the presence of Angel here. [Hey!] Angel protested (of course she does) [You know Yugi trusts everyone!]
“Keep telling yourself that, Angel.” I said and we started arguing. Angel thinks that Yugi is being too open while I think she’s being too cautious and no one's ever heard her really sharing like she means it. 
Yugi is a teenager at the age of 12, and she’s turning 13 this November. She’s (kind of) capable of taking care of herself, and has a large group of friends at school. Being the shortest and also the most childish - [In people’s eyes only! I give them a good impression about Yugi!] - in the friend group, she gets loved and spoiled quite a lot. She seems to be enjoying her student life in New Zealand, but because we’re staying in her mind, me and Angel are the only ones who truly know what she feels like.
Yugi is fully aware of her status and how she looks in people’s eyes. What if I’m not good enough? What if my scores aren’t as what others expected? What ifs and what ifs. She wants to keep her [fake] childish behaviour and high scores at once. She wants to meet the needs and expectations from her classmates, from her teachers and from society. You might be wondering why I didn’t include her parents here. Are you thinking that they are the most supportive, the best parents ever, that Yugi - a person who’s trying hard for her status - has nothing to worry about their expectations for her? Then you’re absolutely wrong. A lot of events lead to this development in her personality, in her way of thinking. But I’ll (or maybe Angel) will tell you about that later on.
