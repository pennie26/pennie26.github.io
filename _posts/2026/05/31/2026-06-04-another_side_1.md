# Part 1: Pride
## Chapter 1: Angel 
  Let’s just say… I’m the nicest person you could ever meet.
  
  [Did you lose your mind? You’re just good at lying.] 
  
  Devil just ruined my moment. Anyway, I’m Yugi’s bright side. The side that is shown to everyone. Let me tell you a bit about Yugi. Well, she is kind, nice and extremely innocent in her primary years. I could still remember the time in Year 5 when her used-to-be-crush gave her the finger just to tease her. It’s clear that she understood the finger means no good, but it was a year later  for her to really fully understand that the finger is an insult, even if the case in Year 5 is just for teasing. 
  
  When her friends in Intermediate made inappropriate jokes, I stopped Devil, or otherwise, her picture of innocence disappeared immediately. 
  
  You can say Yugi is sharp. She sees through mostly everything, she knows when people feel annoyed or when they force a smile. But she never points it out. She either changed her decision so the other person wouldn’t have to do what they don’t want to, even if it means she has to use all of her courage to ask the teacher a question. 
  
  In class, she can be seen as the perfect, the good-in-overall student. The one who isn’t bad at academics, the one who always scores 9s and 10s in every single test and exam. Her chess skill is good, she knows how to solve rubiks, and she’s smart academically and logically. She’s the type of person who everyone looks up to.
