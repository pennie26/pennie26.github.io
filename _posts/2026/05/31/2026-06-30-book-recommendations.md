## Murder Mystery/Crime Genre

- Holly Jackson's books
    - **A Good Girl's Guide To Murder**
    - Good Girl, Bad Blood
    - As Good As Dead
    - Kill Joy

- Richard Osman's books
    - **The Thursday Murder Club**
    - The Man Who Died Twice
    - The Bullet That Missed
    - The Last Devil To Die
    - The Impossible Fortune
 
- Oxford Blood

- Keigo Higashino's books
    - Silent Parade
    - **The Devotion Of Suspect X**
    - Newcomer
    - Malice

## Psychological/Mind games books
I personally really like these type of psychological books. It has less action and/or fighting compares to murder mysteries but carries the same suspense and tension. When reading these type of books, I have a very bad habit of flipping to the next few pages to see what will happen or google it up, which is definitely NOT recommended.

- **Classroom Of The Elite Light Novels**
This series is still ongoing, and it is adapt into a manga and an anime, which season 4 is airing right now. The animations and art is very beautiful, and I love it a lot. For those of you who dislikes looking at long stories, the anime/manga is very good. The light novels have more information and more detail, and if you don't understand something in the anime, the light novel will likely explains it.

- Death Note
This is a series of manga about a 'twisted' world. It's very good and the suspense is SUPER fabulous. There will be a high chance that someone will spoil you what will happens if you were just simply scrolling through Pinterest looking for pictures. Be careful if you don't want to be spoiled. 

## Fantasy Genre
Why, of course, I have a whole closet of recommendations when it comes to fantasy, but I'm not going to waste your time. 

- **A Series of Unfortunate Events**
It's pretty *miserable* and sometimes it's like a comedy if you ask me, but one thing is that each book will keep uncover more and more truths and you will find yourself frustrated because the book ends with you NOT knowing the whatever-secret is in the book. It's kind of like 'there's a piece of paper that could uncovers the painful truth' and then FULL STOP.
Aaaand the book ends.
Something like that. 

- Harry Potter (of course)

- Percy Jackson series (also of course)

- Lynette Noni's books
    - The Prison Healer
    - The Gilded Cage
    - The Blood Traitor
  
 - **The Cat Who Saves Books**
This is an amazing novel, and it carries a lot of meanings. I've read this a dozen times, and every time I re-read it, the stunningness is still the same. 

## Special Books (my favourite ones)

- The Miracles Of The Namiya General Store - by *Keigo Higashino*.
A masterpiece. Unlike his other books that I had listed above, *The Miracles Of The Namiya General Store* (such a long name) is a bright, heart-warming novel about advice and advice seekers. I'm going to do a book talk in English about this.
It have a unique fragmented structure and the characters mainly communicates using letters instead of traditional dialogues. And don't worry, the letter - despite being long - is not boring or dull at all. It's very very very very good. Fabulous. Amazing. Fascinating. Magnificent. Every positive word you could use.
